The Unseen Attack

Spontaneous Coronary Artery Dissection (SCAD) is a leading cause of heart attacks in young women. Yet, it remains dangerously under-diagnosed and under-researched.

An Overwhelmingly Female Condition

Across all major studies, over 90% of SCAD patients are women, a statistic that sets it apart from typical atherosclerotic heart disease.

The Research Gap

Data shows patients are overwhelmingly white (82-95%), highlighting potential underdiagnosis or research gaps in other populations.

"Most large cardiovascular trials exclude pre-menopausal women, creating a dangerous blind spot in medical knowledge." - AHA Scientific Statement (2018)

The Left Anterior Descending (LAD) is Most Commonly Affected

Known as the "widowmaker" artery, the LAD is the site of dissection in half of all SCAD cases.

1 in 5 Survivors Will Have Another SCAD Event

The risk of recurrence is a major concern for patients. Out of 20 survivors, 4 may experience another dissection.

(15-22% reported recurrence rate in major studies)
