What is SCAD?

Spontaneous Coronary Artery Dissection is a tear inside a coronary artery wall that causes heart attack. It’s not caused by plaque buildup. It most often affects healthy, young women — and is frequently misdiagnosed or missed entirely.

SCAD by Gender

SCAD overwhelmingly affects women — but public awareness and medical education often lag behind the data.

Frequently Dismissed

Many SCAD patients are young women with no obvious risk factors. Their symptoms are often dismissed as anxiety or indigestion — leading to dangerous delays in treatment.

Common Triggers

  • Extreme emotional stress
  • Hormonal changes (e.g., post-partum)
  • Intense physical exertion
  • Underlying arteriopathies (e.g., FMD)

Treatment Isn’t Always Clear

Standard heart attack interventions (like stents) may fail or worsen outcomes in SCAD. Many patients benefit more from conservative, non-invasive management — but many doctors aren’t trained to recognize this.

Underfunded. Understudied.

SCAD is underrepresented in cardiovascular research. Most large trials exclude women and younger patients, creating gaps in care, policy, and understanding.
