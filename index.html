The Unseen Attack

Spontaneous Coronary Artery Dissection (SCAD) is a leading cause of heart attacks in young women. Yet, it remains dangerously under-diagnosed and under-researched. This is the story told by the data.

What is SCAD?

Unlike traditional heart attacks caused by plaque, SCAD involves a tear in an artery wall. This can restrict blood flow and cause a heart attack, primarily affecting healthy individuals without standard cardiovascular risk factors.

Chest Pain is the Overwhelming Primary Symptom

While nearly all patients experience chest pain, other common symptoms can be mistaken for less critical conditions, contributing to misdiagnosis.

SCAD Primarily Affects Women in Their 40s and 50s

It is the #1 cause of heart attacks in women under 50 and new mothers.

Because patients don't fit the typical profile of a heart attack victim, their symptoms are often dismissed as anxiety or indigestion, leading to critical delays in care.

Underfunded. Understudied.

Cardiovascular research has historically focused on men. As a result, conditions like SCAD that predominantly affect women are left with significant gaps in understanding, diagnosis, and treatment protocols.

"Most large cardiovascular trials exclude pre-menopausal women, creating a dangerous blind spot in medical knowledge." - AHA Scientific Statement
